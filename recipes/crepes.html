Honey Butter Crêpes

Description

Crêpes are a very thin pancake that can either be served sweet or savory style. The batter itself is not very sweet, only calling for 1 tbsp of sugar for the whole batch.

Required ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk, room temp.
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1/4 tsp salt
    • For the Filling:
  • 6 tbsp butter, softened (room temperature)
  • Raw Clover Honey
  • Powdered Sugar

Instructions

  1. Combine the milk, flour, eggs, sugar, butter, and salt in a blender and process until mixture is fully combined.
  2. Let sit for 15 minutes (or store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a day).
  3. After 15 minutes, stir and heat a 10 to 12 inch non-stick skillet over medium heat. Lightly coat with butter.
  4. Pour 1/3 cup of the batter into the skillet at a time, swirling the pan around to coat in an even layer.
  5. Once the edges are crisped slightly and the center has set (about 30 to 45 seconds), carefully lift the edges of the crêpe and flip over, cooking for an additional 20 to 30 seconds.
  6. Remove from skillet by simply sliding it out onto a plate to keep warm.
    7. Filling the Crêpes:
  7. Spread 1/2 tbsp of room temperature butter onto half of the crêpe, then fold over.
  8. Fold over again, making a small triangle shape.
  9. Drizzle with honey and lightly dust with powdered sugar.
  10. Serve warm. Enjoy!
